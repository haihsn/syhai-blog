The crypto regulatory environment is moving fast. Faster than most traders realize.

I spent a weekend feeding regulatory documents to Claude and asking it to extract everything relevant to retail traders. Here's what matters.

## What's actually legal now

The framework establishes that **cryptocurrency ownership is legal** for individual investors. You can hold BTC, ETH, and other assets without legal risk from simple ownership.

Explicitly permitted:
- Buying and selling on licensed foreign exchanges
- Peer-to-peer transactions between individuals
- Using crypto as investment assets (not payment)

## What's prohibited or unclear

**Using crypto as payment** for goods and services remains prohibited.

**Tax treatment** is the biggest grey area. The obligation to pay tax is clear but the *how* is not yet specified.

## How I'm adjusting

Three practical changes:

**1. Keeping cleaner records.** Every trade, deposit, and withdrawal logged with dates and purpose.

**2. Moving away from unlicensed OTC.** The risk profile just changed.

**3. Watching for implementation decrees.** The framework is the headline — the implementation rules contain the details that actually affect day-to-day trading.

## What Claude found that I missed

Running this through Claude surfaced indirect implications I wouldn't have caught — including language about reporting obligations for large transactions that intersects with existing foreign exchange controls.
