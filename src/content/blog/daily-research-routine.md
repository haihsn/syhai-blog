Most crypto research is noise. Twitter, Telegram, YouTube — everyone has an opinion, nobody has context.

I built a 20-minute morning routine using Claude that filters noise into signal.

## The master prompt

Every morning I paste this into a fresh Claude conversation:

> "Summarize the last 24 hours of significant market developments. Focus on: (1) BTC and ETH price action and key levels, (2) macro events that moved markets overnight, (3) significant on-chain activity or exchange flows, (4) any regulatory news. Under 300 words. Flag the one thing I should watch most closely today."

With web search enabled, Claude pulls current data and gives me a structured briefing in about 90 seconds.

## The deep dive

Whatever Claude flagged as most important, I follow up:

> "Tell me more about [item]. What are the bull and bear interpretations? What would confirm each? What's the historical precedent?"

## The position check

> "Given this market context, are there any conflicts with my current positions? I'm holding [summary]."

This forces me to reconcile my thesis with new information every single day.

## Why it works

The routine separates information gathering from analysis. Claude does the gathering. I do the analysis.

After three months, the biggest insight is how often my prior thesis conflicts with new information — and how easy it is to miss that without a structured daily check.
